# Clinical Signal Map

Clinical Signal Map treats interoperability as a disciplined field of motion: a calm center, measured paths, and precise external systems arranged with purposeful distance. The composition should feel meticulously crafted, with every route tuned through spacing, weight, and rhythm rather than explained through paragraphs.

The palette uses restrained clinical neutrals with distinct protocol colors. Each color acts as a signal lane, not decoration. The visual hierarchy comes from contrast, scale, and sparse typography, all handled with painstaking attention so the result feels like the product of deep expertise.

Forms are soft enough to feel operational and human, but still exact. The central system carries the most weight; connected systems remain quieter satellites. Arrows, labels, and small reference marks carry the workflow meaning with master-level execution and no unnecessary text.

The final image should look presentation-ready at a glance and hold up under closer inspection. Alignment, margins, and label placement must feel labored over with care, as if the diagram were refined across many passes by someone at the top of their field.
